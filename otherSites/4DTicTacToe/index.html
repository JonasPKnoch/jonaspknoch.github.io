Hyper-Tic-Tac-Toe, as the name suggests, is the game of Tic-Tac-Toe played in 4 dimensions. The title comes from the common prefix "hyper" used for 4D versions of objects, like a hyper-cube. 3 in a row are still required to win in Hyper-Tic-Tac-Toe, and they still follow a generalized version of the same rules. 3 in a row is defined as X's or O's that form a continuous straight line. A continuous straight line is defined as a series of squares that are at most one square away in each dimension in a single direction. Below, two dimensions are visualized as the larger octothorpe (Tic-Tac-Toe board), and two more as the smaller octothorpes within each square of the larger one. Each of the smaller boards acts like a normal 2D game of Tic-Tac-Toe, but you can make 3 in a row using the larger boards as well.
